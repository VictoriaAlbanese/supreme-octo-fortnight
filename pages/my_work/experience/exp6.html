Software Engineering Co-op

Symbotic LLC | Wilmington, MA

June 2016 - August 2016 { 3 months }

I am so completely excited to have worked at Symbotic, and am even more excited at the prospect of returning this summer. The people that I met there are so amazing, the facilities are excellent, and the robots are super cool.

I am in the Co-op Scholars program at the University of Massachusetts Lowell, and it is through this program that I got my summer job. I attended an informational session about the company, and had the excellent opportunity to do a one-on-one resume review with Chad Gambino (who later became my manager). After interviewing with some of the head software engineers and touring the facilities, I was offered the job.

During my time here, I created a “Life of an Order” web application, which searches a database and parses log files to display data and metrics on the lifetime of an order through a warehouse automation system. I was responsible for both front-end design with HTML and CSS and back-end development using C#, ASP.NET, Razor, and NancyFX. The project was unit tested to 100% code coverage, utilizing tools like NUnit and Moq.

I learned loads at this company. First and foremost, I learned how important it is to design your program before diving right in. Here, I produced sequence and class diagrams as part of extensive architectural design prior to the project’s start. In addition, I learned how to make my program completely extensible. I also underwent intensive Agile and Scrum training, attending a formal workshop, and then grooming, pointing, and completing stories during two-week sprints using Version One, also participated actively in retrospectives and standups.

I know I learned a lot, and I know I was able to contribute positively and meaningfully to the company. I look forward to being able to do so again!
