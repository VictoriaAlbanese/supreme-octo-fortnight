Engineering Intern

Omtool, Ltd. | Andover, MA

May 2014 - January 2015 { 8 months }

This job was my first experience working at a real company. My uncle, who is a software engineer Omtool, was my mentor for my senior project, and was so happy with my work that at the end I was offered an internship at his company. I worked here the summer after my senior year of high school, and my during my first winter break at college. The breaks in my employment were so I could focus on my education.

During my time at Omtool, in which I was very new to writing code, I helped design, develop, implement, and maintain automated UI tests for existing programs by using QAlibers Test Builder, a drag and drop, slightly outdated test building program. I also participated in weekly SCRUM meetings, and was able to communicate and collaborate insightfully and effectively with my coworkers.

There is so much that I learned at Omtool, the biggest thing perhaps being simply how it feels to work at a company. Having coworkers and interacting with them, and seeing how they interact with each other to fix bugs and build a project and work towards a goal was really cool. Familiarizing myself with all of the stages of writing, reviewing, testing, and pushing code to a version control system was also super valuable. In addition, working primarily with a nearly obsolete program required a lot of dedication, motivation, and independant research, which showed me that I can work successfully on my own to teach myself new skills.
