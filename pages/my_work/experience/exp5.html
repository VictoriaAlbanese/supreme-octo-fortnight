Software Developer

UML Engaging Computing Group | Lowell, MA
February 2016 - Present
{ 5 months and counting! }

I got this job through the recommendation of a friend. After a brief interview, I started working at the lab that very day. I am still working at the lab today, however during the summer, I am taking a break from the lab to get some real world experience at a robotics company.

As a member of the Engaging Computing Group (ECG), a research lab at the University of Massachustees Lowell run by Professor Fred Martin, I am working on helping to update and maintain the iSENSE Project, a web system for collecting, visualizing, and sharing data. I identify and fix bugs in HTML and CSS code, and am gaining practical experience with web development. I was also responsible for crafting a series of video tutorials to replace the extremely outdated and
disorganized former ones.

I was also given the opportunity to present with the ECG at the 2016 USA Science and Engineering Festival (USASEF), giving demos of the website and featured projects to hundreds of parents and children at the event to engage them in and show them the importance
of data visualization.

There's a lot that I'm learning in the ECG lab. I'm mostly learning how to locate bugs in code that I haven't written, and then patch them up. I will also be learning how to use coffescript, a permutation of javascript. Putting my critical thinking to the test to solve problems in this lab has been something that I am grateful for.
