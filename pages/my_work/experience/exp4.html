Research Assistant

UMass Lowell Robotics Lab | Lowell, MA
September 2015 - December 2015
{ 4 months }

This amazing experience was born of my previous co-op work in the robotics lab; when my coworker, Alexander Infantino, and I, were presenting what we had done for the summer, Momotaz Begum approached us to inspect our work and, as her research involved the use of NAO Robots and she liked what she saw, offered us a job doing research under her grad students, which we were only too happy to accept. Our time doing research under her ended after one semester due to a lack of funding.

Working in a team conducting research under Dr. Momotaz Begum in the areas of Intelligent Assistive Technology and Human Robot Interaction, at this job I contributing to the development of a machine learning algorithm which will enable a NAO Robot to autonomously deliver therapy to children with autism spectrum disorders. I did this by programming the NAO Robot in C++ to perform specific tasks which would contribute to the final algorithm, utilizing Robot Operating System (ROS) to accomplish this. At the end of it all, I cowrote a program which harvests and timestamps visual and audio data from the NAO's sensors, and I indepandantly wrote a program which integrates facial recognition and name matching to the nao's cameras, resulting in a cool "Who Am I?" demo.

This semester I really learned how to balance well a full courseload with a part time job. Being able to juggle the stress of classes with the added stress of work was a a challenge, but I really accomplished and learned a lot. I continued to grow comfortable with C++, Linux, ROS, and Github, and in addition, I learned a lot about OpenCV in my work with face detection and recognition.
