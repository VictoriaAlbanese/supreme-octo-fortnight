Robotics Intern

UMass Lowell Robotics Lab | Lowell, MA
June 2013 - August 2014
{ 3 months }

When I was accepted to the University of Massachusetts Lowell, I was awarded a scholarship where I would co-op with one of the professors for 10 weeks over the summer and in turn be paid $4000. I was so beyond excited when a spot at the robotics lab and the opportunity to work under Holly Yanco opened up! As a result of this job,
I got offered my next job during my next
semester in school.

In the lab, my coworker, Alexander Infantino , and I designed and developed programs written in C++ which enable a NAO Robot to detect and react to its environment with its sensors, and to perform specific tasks, including standing up when it has fallen down, avoiding obstacles when navigating a room, walking, talking, and even hula dancing! All of this was implemented utilizing Robot Operating System (ROS) on a Linux operating system.

I learned so many skills at this job. Not only did I really get to excercise my knowledge of coding that I had, by that time, built up, but I really got to expand and refine my ability to work in a linux environment. Learning to use vim and Github was really valuable, and using ROS, the most widely used platform for robots, will definitely come in very useful during the course of my career. Above all, the opportunity to work with robots in such a hands-on environment really enforced to me that robotics is a path that I'm very interested in following.
